Markus Persson

Persson began developing video games at an early age, making games both professionally and for pleasure for much of his life. He achieved critical success upon the publishing of an early version of Minecraft in 2009. Prior to the game's official retail release in 2011, it had sold over ten million copies.[5][6] After this point, Persson stood down as lead designer and transferred creative authority to Jens Bergensten.[7]
